Oblivious transfer and its construction method

发表于 2020-08-05 更新于 2020-08-06 分类于 OT

Oblivious transfer is the very basic tool in secure multiparty computation (MPC), which can be used to construct Yao’s Garbled Circuit and many other useful tools. Famous papers include IKNP2003, C-OT2013.

In this article, we mainly introduce its concepts and its construction method.

Concepts

Imagine such a scenario where Alice holds a pair of private inputs $(m_1,m_2)$ and Bob holds a choice bit $b$. At the end of the protocol, Alice learns nothing about Bob’s choice bit $b$ and Bob learns $m_b$ but learns nothing about $m_{1-b}$. To put it simply, an oblivious transfer can be represented as follow:
$$
(\bot;m_b)\leftarrow OT(m_1,m_2;b)
$$

In the above description, Alice and Bob exchanged their message without revealing any extra imformation to each other besides their own private inputs. In OT, Alice is called Sender and Bob Receiver. Such an OT protocol is called 1-out-of-2 Oblivious Transfer, denoted as $OT^2_1$ meaning one choice bit and 2 messages for choice.

Classical construction of $OT^2_1$

Some common tools

Let $G$ be a cyclic group of prime order $p$ and $g$ its generator.

An RAM is defined as $H:G\to \{0,1\}^n$. In realisation, such an RAM can be instantiated as a hash function like SHA256.

Bellare-Micali Construction

Suppose $A$ has $(x_0,x_1)\in \{0,1\}^n$, and $B$ has $b\in \{0,1\}$.

  1. $A$ samples $c\leftarrow G$, sends it to $B$. $B$ picks $k\leftarrow \Bbb Z_p$, sets $PK_b=g^k, PK_{1-b}=c/g^k$ and sends $PK_0,PK_1$ back to $A$. $A$ checks whether $PK_0PK_0=c$.

  2. $A$ encrypts with EI Gamal using $PK_0,PK_1$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
&C_0=[g^{r_0},H(PK_0^{r_0})\oplus x_0]\\
&C_1=[g^{r_1},H(PK_1^{r_1})\oplus x_1]
\end{aligned}
$$

  then sends $C_0,C_1$ back to $B$

  3. $B$ then decrypts $C_b$, i.e. let $c_0=C_b[0]$ and $c_1=C_b[1]$, then $B$ has $x_b=H(c_0^k)\oplus c_1$.

Security: It’s obvious that $A$ learns nothing during the protocol and $B$ can only learn $x_b$ thus the protocol is secure against semi-honest party.

Noar-Pinkas Construction

[Noar,Pinkas’00] Suppose $A$ has message pair $(m_0,m_1)$ and $B$ has $v\in \{0,1\}$.

  1. $B$ sends the tuple $(g,x=g^a,y=g^b,z_0=g^{c_0},z_1=g^{c_1})$ to $A$, where $a,b\leftarrow \Bbb Z_q,c_v=ab$ and $c_{1-v}\leftarrow \Bbb Z_q$.

  2. $A$ verifies that $z_0\neq z_1$ and applies a partial DDH random self-reduction which computes the following:

$$
\begin{aligned}
&(g,x,y,z_0)\to T_0=(g,x,y_0,z_0^\prime)\\
&(g,x,y,z_1)\to T_1=(g,x,y_1,z_1^\prime)
\end{aligned}
$$
  3. $A$ then encrypts messages using kay pair $(T_0,T_1)$, i.e.
$$
\begin{aligned}
&CT_0=(y_0,m_0z_0^\prime)\\
&CT_1=(y_1,m_1z_1^\prime)
\end{aligned}
$$
  4. $B$ decrypts $CT_v$ to get $m_v$.

Note that DDH random self reduction is not introcuced: future work

$OT^n_1$ from $OT^2_1$

Suppose $A$ holds $(m_0,\dots,m_N)\in \{0,1\}^n$ and B has $t\in \{0,\dots,N\}$. Assume $N=2^l-1$ for some $l$.

  1. $A$ prepares $2l$ keys $(K^0_1,K^1_1),\dots,(K^0_l,K^1_l)$.

  2. Let $F_k:\{0,1\}^l\to \{0,1\}^n$ be an PRF. $A$ sends to $B$ the tuple $(C_0,\dots,C_N)$, i.e. view the message index as a bit string $I=I_1,\dots,I_l\in \{0,1\}^l$ and encrypts message using for below:
$$
C_I=m_I\oplus\oplus^l_{i=1}F_{K_i^{I_i}}(I)
$$
then $A$ sends the tuple to $B$

  3. Let $t=(t_1,\dots,t_l)\in \{0,1\}^l$. Then $l$ $OT^2_1$s are performed where duting the $j$th $OT$, $A$ has $(K_j^0,K_j^1)$ and $B$ has $t_j\in \{0,1\}$.

  4.$B$ now has $K_1^{t_1},\dots,K_l^{t_l}$ and can decrypt $C_t$ to get $m_t$.

Thus with $log N$ $OT_1^2$s, a single $OT_1^N$ can be acheived.
